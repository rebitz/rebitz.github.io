Currently,

I'm a CV Starr fellow at the Princeton Neuroscience Institute, where I work mainly with Tim Buschman and the other fine people in the Buschman lab. I'm also tugging on a few interesting strings with Jon Cohen and Daniel Takahashi at Princeton, and Tirin Moore at Stanford University.

My research

examines how our internal states route sensation into action, and how we recalibrate these states as our environment or goals change. You can read more about that here.

Formerly,

I worked with Michael Platt, then at Duke University, where I got my PhD in Neurobiology. Before grad school, I was a research assistant with Leslie Ungerleider at the NIH.

I spent most of my undergrad years at Simon's Rock, an excellent early college.
