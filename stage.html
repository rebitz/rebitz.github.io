Nous cherchons un·e stagiaire !



The noiseLab is looking for a student intern for summer 2021. This student will work on an NSERC-funded project focused on a fundamental question in human and animal behavior:

why do we make mistakes?

The intern will test the hypothesis that at least some of our mistakes are due to the same mechanisms that help us to explore the world when we are uncertain.

The student's job

will involve collecting behavioral and physiological data from human subjects and processing/analyzing the data in Python or Matlab. If the project is successful, the student will have the opportunity to take the lead on writing up the results to be published in the scientific literature.

The noiseLab

opened in fall 2020 in the Department of Neurosciences. We study how internal goals, like exploration, change how our brains see and interact with the world. We know that a diversity of perspectives improves our work and we encourage applications from people from all backgrounds and with all identities.

you can send an email to Prof. Ebitz. If you would like to apply, please use the PREMIER portal.
